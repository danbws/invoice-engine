The part nobody warns you about

Invoicing looks like CRUD until a tax auditor or a GC's AP department shows up.

Then three things stop being optional: numbers that never skip or repeat, documents that can't be quietly edited after they go out, and cancellations that leave a trail instead of a hole. Spreadsheets fail all three.

Built around the three properties an audit actually checks.

Not features bolted on later. They are the core of the engine, and every one is covered by tests.

Sequential numbers, no duplicates, even under load

The next number is claimed inside the issuing transaction with a row lock on the series counter. Two people hitting issue at the same second serialize instead of racing to the same number.

SELECT … FOR UPDATE

Issued documents are frozen

Drafts edit freely. The moment an invoice gets its number, every mutation is refused. What the GC received is what your records show, byte for byte.

409 Conflict

Cancellation is an event, not a delete

A cancelled invoice keeps its number, records a required reason, and the sequence continues from where it left off. Fiscal numbers are never reused or erased.
